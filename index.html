Projects

CSE 3231 – Database Design Project (Team of 4) AU25

Collaborated in a team of four to develop a comprehensive database system for a mock warehouse company. The project began with designing an ER diagram to model business entities and their relationships, which was then translated into a relational database. We analyzed functional dependencies to ensure the schema was normalized to 3NF/BCNF, guaranteeing data integrity and reducing redundancy.

Using SQLite Studio, we populated the database with sample data and implemented indexes to optimize query performance. To make the database accessible to users, we constructed a Java-based UI connected via JDBC, allowing users to add, modify, delete, and query data efficiently. We also developed transactions to simulate realistic customer interactions and maintain consistency.

Finally, we designed and executed a variety of SQL queries to validate functionality, test performance, and ensure the database accurately supported the intended business operations.

About Me

So let's talk about who I am...

I’m originally from Southgate, Michigan. I grew up with a normal childhood in cookie cutter town, but I grew up with a big personality and and natural talent to do more with my life. Even as a teenager, I was drawn to leadership roles, my first “job” wasn’t even real work. It sounds silly, but at 14, I became a Server Moderator on a Minecraft server with over 1,000 players around the world. I patrolled the server itself, forums, and Discord to keep it family-friendly and running smoothly. I handled ticket requests, managed operations, and learned the thrill of responsibility. No paycheck required, I just loved the challenge.

By 16, it was time for a “real” job, so I joined Tropical Smoothie Cafe, where I spent two years learning teamwork, customer service, and how to keep operations flowing in a fast-paced environment. Those early experiences shaped how I approach challenges today: dive in, take responsibility, and learn from the process.

Then came college, and a very big leap. Out of the four schools I applied to, I chose The Ohio State University, far from home, because I didn’t want the predictable choice. Life had always been structured for me, and for the first time, I didn’t know what to expect. It was scary, it was challenging, it was the unknown and yes, I even considered transferring back. But I stuck it out, switched my major, and learned to embrace uncertainty, lessons that would later prepare me for my internship at Trane Technologies in North Carolina, where I took another leap, but this time it was easier. I spent three months growing both professionally and personally alongside a team of talented and welcoming individuals, which adequately prepared me to start my next chapter in life, post grad.

Now, as a senior at Fisher College of Business, I often reflect on the journey I had taken, and I can say that this is just the beginning of my story, one where curiosity, creativity, and a little bit of fearlessness guide every step.
